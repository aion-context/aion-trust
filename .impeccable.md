# aion-trust — design context

## Design Context

### Users
Buyers and decision-makers — CTOs, heads of HR/talent, heads of security, founders — evaluating
whether to adopt the verifiable-résumé trust layer. They are shown this demo (often voice-narrated
and acted out as a walkthrough), not handed the code. They care about the trust story, the
credibility of the cryptography *as a promise*, and where the money/time savings land — not
implementation detail. Used on a laptop in a meeting / recorded demo, in good lighting.

### Brand Personality
Precise · institutional · trustworthy. The voice is calm and exact, never hype. Proof over
adjectives; transparency (including stated limitations — "local single-operator demo, not a custody
architecture") is a credibility asset, not a caveat to hide. Emotional goal: *quiet confidence* —
"this is rigorous, and I understand exactly what just happened."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined institutional, light.** Warm off-white canvas, ink-near-black text, generous whitespace,
asymmetric (no centered-everything, no card-soup). One confident accent derived from the brand
green — a deep **pine** (the logo's mint `#36d992` deepened for a light background; bright mint
on light is garish). The logo's amber and coral become the natural semantic pair: amber = revoked /
caution, deepened oxblood-coral = rejected / failed, pine-green = accepted / verified. Feels like a
premium printed annual report: exact, serious, warm.

- **Theme:** light (the canvas tinted toward the brand green hue, ~160°).
- **Type:** distinctive serif headlines (Libre Caslon — institutional, Caslon evokes printed
  documents) + clean grotesque body (Hanken Grotesk) + a real mono **only** for identifiers,
  DIDs, and hashes (Spline Sans Mono). No monospace-as-"techy" shorthand.
- **Anti-references:** cyan-on-dark "crypto dApp" look; purple→blue gradients; neon glow; card grids
  with icon-over-heading; gradient text; left-stripe alert bars. None of these appear.

### Design Principles
1. **Credibility is the design problem.** Every claim is shown with its proof (the four checks,
   the signature, the registry standing) — never asserted. Precision in every detail signals rigor.
2. **The verdict is the hero.** ACCEPTED / REJECTED and *why* is the single most important thing on
   the verifier and walkthrough — rank visual attention to match.
3. **Three actors, three clear rooms.** Issuer console, candidate wallet, employer verifier are
   distinct, legible spaces with their own identity — not one undifferentiated dashboard.
4. **Plain language for buyers, exact data on demand.** Lead with the outcome in plain English;
   reveal the cryptographic specifics (hashes, DIDs, audit paths) through progressive disclosure.
5. **Honest about scope.** The local-demo / no-custody nature is stated plainly and tastefully,
   because transparency is the trust pitch.
